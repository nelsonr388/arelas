A Debt of Blood · Chapter IV
The House Burns

Word reaches you in the early hours. Mela's home is ash. Not an accident — a message. Lady Pheressa of House Valo-Danari refused to continue paying her late husband's pension. When Mela pressed the matter, Pheressa sent men with torches.

Mela survived. Barely. She is unhurt but homeless, her thirty years of loyal service rewarded with cinders and silence.

Something cold settles behind your eyes. Not anger — something colder than anger. Pheressa will answer for this.

Your current crew are thanked and paid off. This next move requires specialists. You have three names in mind.
